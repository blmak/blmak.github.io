About the Artist

Heidi Chisholm was born in South Africa, she sees things through African eyes. The colors, patterns and mashups of style that are visible on the Continent has inspired her for many years. In 2008 she moved to Brooklyn from South Africa. It was very hard for her to leave her beloved Africa. It so happened that it was exactly at the time when Obama started running for President and it was the perfect incident to bring her two worlds together. “I designed this bag to celebrates the first black First Lady of the United states by capturing a very sweet moment where she was doing the hula hoop in a public school and advocating for better nutrition.”

About the CFO

Kathryn Koegel marketing, interactive and mobile industry veteran who lives to see the day we have a woman President. Her daughter plays with Heidi’s and she loves the way Heidi juxtaposes African graphics with American imagery. This bag represents the positive vision of the possibility of America that Michelle presents along with her daughters. Kathryn took a sideline into the re:usable bag business after building democracy in Eastern Europe and being fascinated by how everyone carried bags and the streets were NOT littered with plastic. To the issue of NYC banning the bag, Kathryn says: “Bring it Bill DeBlasio – we have artists in Brooklyn standing by to create custom ones for every need.”
